GREENHOUSE DESIGN

This project was my final architecture studio final for my second year in the ENVD program at University of Colorado. This design was for the users and student of our East Campus area. I created a passive building that also incorporated a classroom. The classes would be focused on botany and growing food, so including a green house would benefit these classes. I also made the building passive with the use of these plants in the greenhouse portion of the space, which creates heat. There are different aspects for the heat distribution, such as sky light windows to release heat, black-out blinds and curtains to block the sun to cool the area, a green roof to regulate temperatures, and windows to the classroom that close to adjust to the temperature. There was also an outdoor portion of the design, which included a reflecting pool and seating for meditation or studying. The space is meant to be integrated with the natural surroundings and be a peaceful place for learning.
